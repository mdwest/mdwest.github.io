Do you work on Concrete Floors?

If you have ever spent a day working on concrete flooring, you know the fatigue it causes. But most donâ€™t recognize the cumulative effects of this repetitive trauma. Decades spent in these working conditions can erode your joints, leaving you in chronic pain and potentially eligable for monetary compensation.

Even in the short term, many workers suffer from Shin Splints, Stress Fractures, Lumbar Strain and more. Without any give, or shock absorption, energy from each step travels up your body and can harm the cartilage in your joints and strain your bones. Healthfully recently (2017) advised homeowners to avoid residential concrete flooring due to these risk. And ShoeAdviser recently (2018) noted anyone walking on concrete should consider investing in shock-absorbing footwear.

In Simpson Timber Co. v. Wentworth, Washington State Courts recognized that “Prolonged standing or movement on cement floors may cause an occupational disease.” If you work on concrete and suffer from joint pain, or other musculoskeletal disorders I urge you to contact my office to schedule a free consultation.
