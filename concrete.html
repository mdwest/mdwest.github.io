Do you work on Concrete Floors?

You may have a right to monetary compensation

If you have ever spent a day working on concrete, you know how sore it made your feet. But most don’t know those sore feet add up to serious damage. Decades spent working on concrete floors can damage your joints through a process like erosion, leaving you in chronic pain and may be eligible for medical help and money to compensate for the harm.



Even in the short term, many workers suffer from Shin Splints, Stress Fractures, Lumbar Strain and more. Since concrete doesn't have any give, or shock-absorption, energy from each step travels up your body and can harm the cartilage in your joints and strain your bones. Healthfully recently (2017) advised homeowners to avoid residential concrete flooring due to these risk. And ShoeAdviser recently (2018) noted anyone walking on concrete should consider buying shock-absorbing footwear.

In Simpson Timber Co. v. Wentworth, Washington State Courts recognized that “Prolonged standing or movement on cement floors may cause an occupational disease.” A few common occupational disease symptoms include foot pain, knee pain, hip pain, and back pain, or general joint pain. And many working on concrete flooring are diagnosed with arthritis, osteoarthritis, or shin splints. If you or a loved one work on concrete and suffer from joint pain, or other musculoskeletal disorders, I urge you to contact my office to schedule a free consultation. My office may be able to get you the help and money you deserve.
