Contingency Fees: An Option for Bootstrapping an Equity Crowdfunding Campaign

Photo in the Pubic Domain

If you read my earlier post on equity crowdfunding, you are aware that Washington has a new law allowing businesses to raise up to one-million dollars from Washington residents. This means you can raise investment from friends, family, customers, colleges, and any other Washington State resident.

However, equity crowdfunding is a substantial undertaking; involving a marketing campaign, thousands of dollars in legal work and ongoing reporting requirements. This article looks at the contingency fee arrangement as an option to reduce up-front costs and minimize the risk of an equity crowdfunding campaign.

Contingency fees have a bad reputation. Most people think of ambulance chasing lawyers taking one-third of their client’s settlement. This is abusive and isn’t our model for equity crowdfunding. Instead, we use contingency fees to defer payment of our advertised flat-fees. We don’t need to charge a premium for this deferral because we mitigate our risk by reviewing prospective clients' marketing plan and only accepting clients we believe will succeed.

Together, our deferred contingency fees arrangement has three key benefits.

1) The arrangement reduces the up-front cost of launching an equity crowdfunding campaign.

2) The arrangement reduces the risk of launching a campaign, ensuring you aren’t stuck with thousands of dollars in legal fees if your campaign fails.

3) The arrangement aligns our interests. We only get paid if you succeed. And we hope to continue providing legal services as you scale your business.

Best of all, if you have an incorporated business, with the capacity to produce GAAP compliant financial, and the capacity to manage a marketing campaign, you could file to launch your campaign for as little as $1,000 in up-front costs. If this is beyond your means, but you have a great idea, you may consider filing for an Awesome Foundation Grant to help cover up-front costs.
