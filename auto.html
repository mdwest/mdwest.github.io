Know Your Rights

Dealers often incorporate false terms in contracts.

Car dealerships that employ high-pressure sales tactics often make deals on the fly without writing them into the contract. Often this involves the downpayment. For example, the dealer may agree to a lower downpayment, agree to accept the downpayment in installments or accept the downpayment when you get paid next. If we can prove any part of your contract was false or misleading we can get you a full refund.

Dealers May Fail to Disclose Loan Terms

Under the Truth in Lending Act dealers are required to make full and correct disclosures about car financing. Often they rush you through these documents, so it is important we review copies of anything you signed. However, if they made any mistakes, or failed to make full disclosures, we may be able to get you a full refund.

Failing to Disclose Past Damage to the Vehicle

If the dealer knew a used vehicle was in an accident, flood, or fire, but failed to disclose this, you may be eligable for a refund.

Falsely Stating that Optional Features, Warranties, or Service Fees are Required

Dealers often pack car sales with optional fees that can add up to thousands of dollars. In many cases a single call can force a dealer to remove these fees from the contract and refund the associated costs.

Yo-Yo Sales (a.k.a. Bushing Scam or Spot-Delivery)

When buying a car most people don’t realize the contract is contingent on the dealership securing financing. Dealers often make deals they don’t intend to honor and require the customer to return to the dealership to renegotiate. This tactic is often used to scam customers and if the dealership didn’t follow the exact letter of the law you may have a right to a full refund.

Mitch stands up to shady car dealerships. In the past few years, there has been a spike in predatory lending and sub-prime auto loans. Often, dealerships are pushing cars they know the customer can’t afford, planning to repossess the vehicle in the future.

Example Claims

Cases

Preditory Lending

Dealers often advertise no-money-down, no credit check, or preapproved financing to low-income customers. This is a red flag and often bait to scam customers. If you responded to one of these advertisements contact me to review your sales contract. You may have a right a refund and more.

Yo-Yo Sale

Often dealers will close a deal they know they can not finance in hopes of negotiating a new deal later. Sometimes the dealer will wait a week or more before informing the customer that they failed to secure financing. This is always illegal, but dealers often contact customers within a few days and attempt to renegotiate the contract within the law. However, dealers are required to notify the customer that the initial contract is void and tender a full refund. If you were asked to return to the dealer because your financing fell through, you may be the victim of a scam.

Language Scams

If English is not your first language, you are at particular risk for dealership scams. Dealers often negotiate in one language but require you to sign contracts in english. Often theses contract have unfavorable terms. If you signed a contract in a second language, be sure to have it reviewed for scams.
